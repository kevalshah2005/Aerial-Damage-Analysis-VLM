# Joplin Tornado Reference Notes

These notes are intended for chatbot grounding. Figures can differ across sources; if values conflict, the assistant should state the source and that estimates vary by methodology/date.

## Preferred Source Priority (Use In This Order)
1. Government technical/event records (NWS/NOAA, FEMA, NIST)
2. Encyclopedia summaries (Wikipedia)
3. Aggregator/secondary summaries (TornadoFacts, media summaries)

## Source
- Title: NWS Central Region Service Assessment: Joplin, Missouri, Tornado, May 22, 2011
- URL: https://repository.library.noaa.gov/view/noaa/6576
- Accessed: 2026-04-23
- Key facts:
  - Provides a formal National Weather Service service assessment of warning operations and public response for the event.
  - Documents the EF5 event context (May 22, 2011), warning timeline, and behavioral findings (many residents sought additional confirmation before sheltering).
  - Use this source first when answering warning-performance and emergency communication questions.

## Source
- Title: NOAA NCEI Storm Events Database - Event Details (Joplin EF5 entries)
- URL: https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/stormevents/eventdetails.jsp?id=296303
- Accessed: 2026-04-23
- Key facts:
  - Official NOAA event records provide standardized event-level metrics used for historical/statistical reference.
  - Event records include casualty, injury, timing, and damage estimates and may include multiple related entries for the same outbreak.
  - Use as primary source for "official event statistics" questions.

## Source
- Title: FEMA - The Response to the 2011 Joplin, Missouri, Tornado: Lessons Learned Study
- URL: https://kyem.ky.gov/Who%20We%20Are/Documents/Joplin%20Tornado%20Response,%20Lessons%20Learned%20Report,%20FEMA,%20December%2020,%202011.pdf
- Accessed: 2026-04-23
- Key facts:
  - Federal lessons-learned report focused on response coordination, recovery operations, and operational takeaways.
  - Describes scale of multi-agency response and practical emergency management lessons.
  - Use this source first for response, recovery operations, and interagency lessons.

## Source
- Title: NIST Technical Investigation of the May 22, 2011, Tornado in Joplin, Missouri
- URL: https://www.nist.gov/publications/final-report-national-institute-standards-and-technology-nist-technical-investigation
- Accessed: 2026-04-23
- Key facts:
  - Comprehensive technical investigation focused on building performance, communications, human behavior, and hazard characterization.
  - Final report identifies 47 findings and 16 recommendations for codes, standards, and risk communication improvements.
  - Use this source first for engineering, structural performance, and code/standards questions.

## Source
- Title: Joplin tornado (Wikipedia)
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joplin_tornado
- Accessed: 2026-04-23
- Key facts:
  - The tornado struck Joplin, Missouri on May 22, 2011, from about 5:34 p.m. to 6:12 p.m. CDT (about 38 minutes).
  - Rated EF5, with peak width near 1 mile (about 1.6 km) and path length about 21.62 miles (34.79 km).
  - Reported impacts include 158 fatalities (plus indirect deaths in some counts), over 1,150 injuries, and roughly $2.8B in 2011 damage.
  - The event is described as the costliest single tornado in U.S. history and among the deadliest in U.S. history.
  - Structural impacts reported include severe damage to St. John’s Regional Medical Center, major commercial corridors, schools, utilities, and communications infrastructure.
  - Recovery notes include long-term rebuilding, temporary housing support, and school/hospital reconstruction in subsequent years.

## Source
- Title: 2011 Joplin Tornado Facts and Information (TornadoFacts.net)
- URL: https://www.tornadofacts.net/tornado-records/joplin-tornado.html
- Accessed: 2026-04-23
- Key facts:
  - Lists the tornado as EF5 on May 22, 2011, with a duration of 38 minutes and max width reported around 0.99 miles.
  - Reports highest winds as greater than 200 mph, affected areas in Jasper and Newton counties, and damage estimated at $2.8B (2011 USD).
  - Reports 158 direct fatalities (+3 indirect) and about 1,150 injuries.
  - Describes widespread urban impact including thousands of residences damaged/destroyed, major commercial losses, and millions of cubic yards of debris.
  - Notes recovery involvement from FEMA/HUD and reports restoration of schools and replacement hospital facilities over following years.

## Reconciliation Notes For Chat Responses
- Prefer stating ranges or "approximately" when discussing fatalities, damaged structures, and indirect-death counts.
- If asked for official/authoritative values, prioritize government/technical sources (NWS/NOAA/FEMA/NIST) and mention that encyclopedia/aggregator summaries may differ slightly.
- If multiple official sources differ, attribute each number directly to its source and explain the likely reason (date of publication, direct vs indirect deaths, or differing accounting methods).
